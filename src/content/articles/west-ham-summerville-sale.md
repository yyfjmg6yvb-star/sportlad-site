Crysencio Summerville's had interest from Manchester United, Chelsea, and Fulham this summer, with a fee reportedly around £50m on the table. Meanwhile Axel Disasi looks close to arriving the other way for around £25m once Chelsea's side of that deal is sorted.

So the pattern, again: sell the good one, buy a replacement-level one, pocket the difference, call it "smart business."

I'm not going to pretend that's not sometimes the right call for a club West Ham's size — nobody's asking them to outbid Manchester United on wages for a player who clearly fancies the move. But here's my actual gripe: this exact story has played out at West Ham enough times now that it's stopped being a one-off and started being the identity. Sell the standout, reinvest sensibly, stay mid-table, repeat.

That's a completely fine way to run a football club if you're honest that it's what you're doing. What gets me is when it's dressed up as ambition every single summer, and then quietly forgotten about every single August when the best player's gone and the "exciting replacement" turns out to be, at best, a lateral move.

If Summerville goes for £50m, I want to see genuinely ambitious reinvestment, not just a swap that balances the books. Anything less and West Ham fans are entitled to be properly fed up — not just resigned to it, which is what this always seems to turn into by the time deadline day rolls around.

**Where I could be wrong:** if that £50m gets thrown at two or three real upgrades rather than one so-so one, this take flips completely — and West Ham do have a decent recent record of reinvesting sensibly when they actually get paid what a player's worth.
